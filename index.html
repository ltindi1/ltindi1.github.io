Useful academic information to know are: student-faculty ratio

There's a lot to consider before making a decision to which university you will be attending. The first is the student-faculty ratio. This is the number of students attending the university divided by the number of teachers. Lower ratios will guarantee more individualized attention from the teacher.

tuition

Tuition is another figure potential students should look at before making a decision on a university. Nowadays universities are exponentially continuing to be more expensive and knowing the price tag to a college is the first step in planning how to pay for it. There are a number of scholarships and financial aid that can help with paying for the school you're planning on attending.

And lastly, location

The last figure to look at before choosing the right university is the location. Many of us are accustomed to rural areas, while others to suburban and the rest to urban. Some of us want to be close to home while others far away. Knowing the location of the university can play a major role in the experience you'll have in the four years as a student.
